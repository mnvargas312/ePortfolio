About Me

I am a Learning Design and Technology graduate student with a strong foundation in computer science, mathematics education, and instructional design. My work focuses on the place where teaching, technology, and user experience come together. I am committed to building learning experiences that are meaningful, accessible, and designed with intention. My background supporting multilingual learners and teaching Algebra 1 and Geometry has shaped the way I think about equity, clarity, and how digital tools can remove barriers for students.

My interests include learning design, educational technology, math pedagogy, user experience for instructional platforms, and data informed decision making. I enjoy creating digital tools, developing curriculum, and designing systems that make learning easier to navigate for both students and teachers. I have experience building web applications, collaborating with educators, and integrating technology into standards aligned lessons that improve engagement and understanding.

My long term goal is to grow as an instructional designer and eventually lead technology initiatives in either K through 12 education or higher education. I hope to design solutions that scale, create tools that support teacher workflow, and build digital experiences that make learning more equitable and engaging. I want to use my background in computer science, education, and design to create learning environments that help students feel confident and supported. I am especially focused on developing tools that meet the needs of multilingual learners, since their success often depends on thoughtful design choices that make content more accessible. My goal is to create learning experiences that help all students reach their full potential.
