Featured Artifacts

Podcast – Objective 1

SUMMARY

This short podcast introduces practical negotiation strategies for purchasing a car at a dealership, with a focus on how to haggle for a fair price. In the episode, I walk listeners through key ideas such as setting a target price, using initial offers strategically, and recognizing when to step away from a deal. I scripted and recorded the episode to be concise, approachable, and clearly organized so that a general audience can follow the concepts even if they are unfamiliar with negotiation or financial terminology.

Interactive Module – Objective 2

SUMMARY

This interactive module teaches the basic rules and goals of Blackjack and guides learners in applying basic strategy to decide whether to hit or stay based on the hand they are dealt. The module combines short explanations, step-by-step examples, and scenario-based practice where learners make decisions and immediately see the consequences of their choices. The goal is to help learners connect intuitive decision-making with probability-informed strategies in a low-stakes, game-based environment.

AI Tutor Agent – Objective 3

SUMMARY

This AI tutoring agent was developed with Microsoft Copilot to help Algebra 1 students prepare for their Unit 3 assessment on equations and inequalities. Rather than providing direct answers, the agent guides students through the problem-solving process by asking questions, diagnosing misconceptions, generating personalized practice problems, and providing adaptive feedback that encourages productive struggle and deeper understanding.

Instructional Design Project – Objective 4

SUMMARY

This instructional design project focused on creating a job-embedded professional learning experience for secondary school Collaborative Team (CT) facilitators. The goal of the training was to address inconsistent facilitation practices and improve the effectiveness of CT meetings through structured agendas, data-analysis protocols, facilitation language, and actionable next steps. I designed the project around authentic school-based contexts by using real CT materials, realistic meeting constraints, and practical facilitation tools that participants could immediately apply in their own departments.
